Key findings

FINDING 01

The M1–M2 cancellation cliff

~18% of new acquisitions arrive already unhappy. They had a bad first impression, payment friction, or product mismatch — and they leave within 60 days. This is the single largest retention opportunity.

FINDING 02

NPS is the dominant early-churn signal

73% of month-2 churners are detractors (NPS ≤ 6) vs 25% of retained subscribers — a 3.0× higher detractor rate. The signal shows up at day 30, giving the team time to act.

FINDING 03

A simple model captures it at scale

Logistic-regression classifier on a day-30 snapshot predicts 90-day churn at 78% precision (67% recall, AUC 0.84). Top features: NPS, payment failures, swap requests. Flags ~29% of the base.

FINDING 04

Channel quality varies sharply

Referral and Direct subscribers have ~25% higher 12-month LTV than Meta Ads. Lower CAC too. There’s a clear marketing-mix reallocation opportunity.

Recommendation

MONTH-2 SAVE PLAN

Trigger a loyalty credit + free Swap for subscribers flagged at day 30

Mirrors the existing Swap / Relocation programs, applied earlier in tenure where the unit economics are most damaged. Run as A/B test: 50% flagged subscribers get the intervention, 50% control. Measure incremental retention and LTV over 90 days; scale if incremental LTV per saved subscriber > intervention cost.

Method & stack

How it’s built

  1. Data simulation. 12,000 subscribers with a latent satisfaction score driving both NPS and churn — the same way real customer behaviour works.
  2. Cohort retention. Per-cohort survival from acquisition month, observed through May 2026.
  3. LTV. Cumulative ARPU × retention, 18-month horizon, broken out by channel.
  4. Churn classifier. scikit-learn LogisticRegression on day-30 features; threshold tuned on the PR curve to hit 78% precision.
  5. Dashboard. Matplotlib / seaborn, BI-style layout, exported as PNG + PDF. Production deployment lifts directly into Power BI or Tableau against a live warehouse.
